Vegetarian Chili

Description

This contest winning vegetarian chili makes a huge pot to feed the entire family. Packed with veggies, beans, and a ton of flavor. No one would know it's vegetarian. Super easy to make in one pot.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium zucchini,chopped
  • 2 medium onions,chopped
  • 1 medium green pepper,chopped
  • 1 medium sweeet pepper,chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 cans (28 ounces each) Italian stewed tomatoes, cut up
  • 1 can (15 ounces) tomatoe sauce
  • 1 can (15 ounces) pinto beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1/4 cup minced fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 minced fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablepoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin

Instructions

  1. In a Dutch oven, cook zucchini, onions and peppers in oil over medium-high heat until tender. Add garlic; cook 1 minute.
  2. Stir in remaining ingredients. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat; cover and simmer 30 minutes or until heated through, stirring occasionally.

Vegan Brownies

Description

These brownies are gooey, sweet, and studded with chocolate chunks. They're definitely my favorite brownie recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup flour
  • 2/3 cup cocoa/cacao powder
  • 1 1/4 cups sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup non-dairy butter
  • 3 flax eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • generous pinch of salt
  • 2 oz. dark chocolate bar, chopped
  • coarse salt, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Line and 8 x 8 baking dish with parchment paper, or lightly grease with oil. If using a 9 x 13 baking dish, see recipe instructions below.
  2. Flax egg: In a small bowl, combine 3 heaped tablespoons flaxseed meal with 1/2 cup water or unsweetened non-dairy milk. Let rest for 10 minutes to thicken.
  3. Mix: In a medium size mixing bowl, add the 1/2 cup butter/oil/applesauce and sugar, mix well. Add in the flax eggs and vanilla, mix again. Add in the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder and salt, mix well to combine. May take a minute or so to completely become mixed.
  4. Pour: Pour brownie batter into the center of the prepared baking dish. Spread to the edges as needed. Optionally top with chopped chunks of dark chocolate or vegan chocolate chips.
  5. Bake: Place in the center of the oven and bake for 25 – 30 minutes.
  6. Cool: Let cool in the baking dish. If you used parchment paper, after 5 – 10 minutes, lift the edges and move the brownies to a wire rack to cool.
  7. Store: Keep loosely covered on the counter for up to 4 – 5 days. They keep longer in the refrigerator, up to 2 weeks.
